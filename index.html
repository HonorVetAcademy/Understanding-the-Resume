Resume Anatomy

Every healthcare resume has a predictable structure. Learn to navigate it at a glance. Use the annotated sample on the left — based on a real RN resume — alongside the recruiter callouts on the right.

Lucy Obiozor, RN
📞 904-532-2949  |  ✉ luluog5@gmail.com  |  📍 Jacksonville, FL 32218
① Professional Summary
Compassionate RN with Med-Surg/Telemetry and Cardiothoracic Step-Down experience in a high-acuity teaching hospital. Skilled in post-op open-heart/thoracic surgery patients. Proficient in Epic EMR. Employee of the Quarter recipient.
② Education
ASN — Florida State College at Jacksonville  Aug 2023
③ Licenses & Certifications
RN License – FL (NLC Compact) Exp: Apr 30, 2027

BLS – AHA ACLS – AHA NIHSS – AHA
All certs expire 2027
④ Work Experience
Baptist Medical Center of Jacksonville — RN Med-Surg/Tele (Staff)
Feb 2024 – Present | Teaching / STAC | Trauma Level II | 900 beds | Ratio 1:4-5 | Epic

Palm Garden of Jacksonville — RN Geriatric & Rehab (Staff)
Dec 2023 – Feb 2024 | SNF | 120 beds

① Summary — Read First

Quickly tells you the specialty, acuity level, and years of experience. Look for keywords: Tele, ICU, L&D, Step-Down, Float. This saves you from reading the whole resume blindly.

② Education — Degree Matters

ASN vs BSN vs MSN affects client eligibility. Many Magnet hospitals require BSN. Check graduation year — a 2023 grad is a new nurse (under 1 year experience).

③ Licenses — Critical Section

Confirm: RN is active, state matches job location OR NLC compact applies, expiry is not within 90 days. Missing expiry date = always ask the candidate.

④ Experience — The Core

Look for: facility type, bed size, patient ratio, EMR system, dates, and whether it's Staff vs Travel vs Agency. Gaps in dates are a red flag to explore.

NLC Compact License

This RN holds an NLC compact license — she can work in any of the 40+ compact states without getting a new license. Critical for travel assignments. Always verify on nursys.com.

// Section 02

Key Resume Sections

Each section of a healthcare resume serves a specific recruiting purpose. Know what to look for — and what questions to ask when something's missing.

👤
Contact Information
Must-have at the top
  • Full legal name (as on license)
  • Phone number (cell preferred)
  • Professional email address
  • Current city and state (ZIP helpful)
  • LinkedIn optional but positive
Missing city/state? Always ask before submitting — some clients won't consider out-of-state without relocation confirmation.
📝
Professional Summary
2–5 lines at the top
  • Specialty (Tele, ICU, OR, L&D…)
  • Years of experience
  • Facility type (teaching, trauma, SNF)
  • Key skills or certifications
  • EMR system proficiency
A strong summary tells you in 10 seconds whether to read on or move to the next candidate.
🎓
Education
Degree type matters
  • Degree: ASN / ADN / BSN / MSN
  • School name and location
  • Graduation date (month + year)
  • BSN required by Magnet hospitals
  • New grad = graduated within 12 months
Never assume degree level — it directly affects which clients will accept the candidate.
🪪
Licenses & Certifications
Non-negotiable verification
  • RN License: state, number, expiry
  • NLC Compact status (multistate?)
  • BLS — always required
  • ACLS, PALS, NRP, NIHSS as applicable
  • Specialty certs: CCRN, CEN, RNC-OB…
Verify all licenses on nursys.com before submission. Never rely solely on what's written on the resume.
🏥
Work Experience
The most critical section
  • Facility name, city, state
  • Employment type: Staff / Travel / Agency
  • Facility type: teaching, STAC, SNF, LTC
  • Bed size & patient ratio (e.g. 1:4–5)
  • EMR system (Epic, Cerner, Meditech)
  • Dates: MM/YYYY – MM/YYYY or Present
No bed size or ratio on the resume? Add it after your intake call with the candidate.
⚙️
Skills / Technology
Optional but helpful
  • EMR/EHR platforms listed here or in experience
  • Clinical skills (IV therapy, vents, drips)
  • Languages spoken (bilingual advantage)
  • Soft skills less relevant — focus on clinical
Clients often filter by specific EMR. If a candidate has Epic experience, make sure it's clearly visible in your formatted version.
// Section 03

HonorVet Format Standards

When you submit a candidate to a client, the resume represents HonorVet. Here's how to format it correctly — and what must always be present.

Why We Reformat Resumes

Candidate resumes come in all shapes — some are 6 pages long, some have photos, some have missing dates, and some use tables that break in ATS systems. Our job is to standardize it into a clean, complete, client-ready document that highlights the candidate's clinical strengths and gives the client everything they need to make a decision.

1

Header — Clean Contact Block

Name (as on license), phone, email, city/state. Remove home address for privacy. Remove photos — they're not professional in US healthcare staffing.

NamePhoneEmailCity, State
2

Summary — Rewrite If Needed

Must lead with specialty and years of experience. If the candidate's summary is vague or missing, write a brief 3-line version from their experience section. Keep it factual — no fluff.

SpecialtyYOEKey SkillsEMR
3

Licenses — Complete Every Field

License type, issuing state, license number, expiry date. BLS/ACLS must show issuing body (AHA) and expiry. NLC compact status must be noted.

TypeStateNumberExpiryNLC?
4

Experience — Add Missing Details

Each role must include: facility name, city/state, employment type (Staff/Travel/Agency), facility type, bed size, patient ratio, EMR, and start/end dates in MM/YYYY format. Get missing details from the candidate during intake.

FacilityTypeBedsRatioEMRDates
5

Length — 1 to 2 Pages Maximum

Remove redundant bullet points. Consolidate older experience (5+ years) into one line. Remove objective statements, references sections, and "References Available Upon Request." Keep it tight.

Max 2 PagesRemove FluffNo References
6

Final Check Before Submission

Proofread for spelling and formatting. Verify license on nursys.com. Confirm candidate's current location and availability. Remove any personal identifiers beyond contact info. Save as PDF.

Spell Checknursys.comSave as PDF
// Format Guide

Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do This
Use MM/YYYY format for all dates (e.g. Feb 2024 – Present)
List the EMR system for every job (Epic, Cerner, Meditech)
Include bed size and patient ratio for every hospital role
Note NLC Compact status if the license is multistate
Keep bullet points clinical and specific to the unit
Verify all license/cert expiry dates before submitting
Save the final formatted resume as a PDF for submission
Note facility type (Teaching, STAC, SNF, LTC, Critical Access)
✗ Avoid This
Submit a resume with expired certifications or licenses
Leave dates as just a year — always use month and year
Include photos, colored backgrounds, or fancy graphics
Send a resume longer than 2 pages to a client
Leave gaps in employment without a note or explanation
Include "References Available Upon Request" — wastes space
Use vague bullet points like "Provided excellent patient care"
Submit without confirming the candidate is still available
// Section 04

Red Flags to Watch For

Not every resume is submission-ready. These are the warning signs that need investigation before you move forward with a candidate.

🚩 Expired License or Certification

If the RN license or BLS/ACLS is expired or expiring within 90 days, the candidate cannot be placed. Always check nursys.com and ask for renewal documents before presenting.

🚩 Unexplained Employment Gaps

A gap of 3+ months between jobs needs explanation. Travel gap? Personal leave? Back to school? Document the reason in your notes. Clients notice gaps and will ask.

🚩 Very Short Tenures (Under 3 Months)

Multiple jobs lasting 1–2 months may signal contract non-completion, terminations, or contract abandonment. Explore this during your intake call before submitting.

⚠️ No EMR Listed Anywhere

Most hospitals require prior Epic or Cerner experience. If no EMR is listed, ask the candidate directly. A new-grad with zero EMR exposure may not meet client requirements.

⚠️ License State Mismatch

The candidate's license state does not match the job location AND the license is not compact (NLC). They'd need to apply for a new state license — timeline is 4–12 weeks. Factor this in.

⚠️ Less Than 1 Year Experience

Most travel/contract positions require a minimum of 1–2 years of recent acute care experience. A 2023 new grad applying in mid-2024 is technically eligible — but many clients will decline.

📋 Missing Facility Details

No bed size, no patient ratio, no facility type. These are non-negotiable for healthcare staffing. Get them from the candidate during intake — without them, the resume can't be submitted.

📋 Staff vs. Travel Not Specified

A candidate who lists 10 jobs in 5 years is likely a traveler. A traveler listing jobs without identifying them as travel looks like a job hopper. Always clarify and label each position correctly.

What does NLC Compact mean on a nursing license?
